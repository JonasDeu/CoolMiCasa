How CoolMiCasa works

The trick to cooling a flat without AC is timing: let cool air in only when it's cooler outside than inside, and seal the place up and block the sun the rest of the time.

  1. Set your location — fetches the real hourly outdoor temperature, sun, and wind for your spot (via the free Open-Meteo API).
  2. Draw your flat — add rooms (include the hallway), place windows on walls with the Window tool, then use the Door tool to connect adjacent rooms. Doors let the app trace how air flows from room to room.
  3. Read the airflow — when it's cooler outside, blue arrows show the cross-breeze path (intake window → through open doors → exhaust window). Rooms off the path are flagged stagnant.
  4. Say how many portable fans you have — the app ranks the best spots and heights for exactly that many: exhaust high where hot air pools (stack effect), intake low for cool night air, and a doorway booster where air accelerates through the constriction (Bernoulli/continuity). Numbered dashed ghosts on the map show where each fan goes.
  5. Rotate North so the compass matches reality. The app computes exactly which windows the sun hits, hour by hour.
  6. Type the temperature you measure in each room (and optionally right at a window).
  7. Read the Right now and Next 24h panels. Green = open & ventilate, orange = keep closed, yellow = sun is hitting that glass so close the shade.

Everything is saved in your browser. No account, nothing leaves your machine except the weather lookup.
